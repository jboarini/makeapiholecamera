Developing the Negative

Developing your exposures is your next step. This is not a complicated process, and is in many ways the most exciting moment, where you will find out if your work to make the camera, choose the scene and make an exposure worked out how you imagined.

You need access to a room you can darken, ideally with a faucet. Usually a bathroom works. It doesnâ€™t need to be pitch black because of the low sensitivity of photo paper, but you should ensure all windows and bottoms of doors are sealed so no light comes in. Developing at night makes this much easier.

Materials You Need

  1. Developing trays (3): These are plastic containers that will serve as a bath for each of the main chemicals. I suggest an 8x10 size at least, but any plastic container will do.
  2. Developer: This chemical will develop the exposed silver in the photo paper.
  3. Stop bath: As the name implies, it stops the development by neutralizing the process.
  4. Fixer: Removes any unexposed silver and adds permanence to the image.
  5. Tongs, so as not to use our fingers.
  6. Safe light bulb: This is to illuminate our work area, and is safe to use because photo paper is insensitive to red light.
  7. Graduated Cylinder: For precise measuring of liquids.
  8. Clothespins

1. Mix Chemicals & Prepare Work Area

Mixing the chemichals is a simple affair. Simply follow dilution directions in the bottle. In our case, we're using Arista, so we will follow these ratios to make half a liter of chemistry for each tray. The first tray on the left will hold the developer, the middle one the stop bath, and the final one is for fixer.

Once the image is fixed, you can place the negative in a bath of fresh water to wait for final rinsing if you are developing multiple sheets. Otherwise, place the sheet in a clean tray and run with tap water for several minutes to ensure all the fixer is removed. Hang on clothesline to dry.

Creating a Positive Print

Your next task is to create a positive. This will be done by scanning your image and inverting in Photoshop. In addition you will further edit , retouch and crop as needed to obtain your final image!
